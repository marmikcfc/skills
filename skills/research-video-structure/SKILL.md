# Research video structure

A research video is not a list of facts. It should answer a real question, show why the answer is contested or non-obvious, and leave the viewer with a compressed mental model.

Use this for prompts like:
- "deep research video on..."
- "make a video essay about..."
- "compare these approaches..."
- "what is really happening in..."
- "state of the art in..."

## The 5 beats

### 1. Question
Open with the concrete question the video will resolve. The question should be narrow enough that a short video can answer it.

Good:
- "Are AI agents actually replacing workflows, or just wrapping chatbots in automation?"
- "Why did this technology look inevitable, then stall?"

Avoid broad openings like "Let's explore the history of..." unless the video is explicitly historical.

### 2. Landscape
Map the competing explanations, stakeholders, or schools of thought. The viewer should understand the shape of the debate before hearing the answer.

The shape: "There are three ways people explain this. Each gets one thing right and one thing wrong."

### 3. Evidence
Show the strongest evidence, not every fact collected. Prefer 2-4 concrete anchors: numbers, examples, papers, product shifts, timelines, or expert claims.

Every evidence point must earn its place by changing the viewer's model.

### 4. Synthesis
Resolve the tension with the video's thesis. This is not a summary; it is the point of view that emerges from the evidence.

The shape: "The real shift is not X. It is Y, because..."

### 5. Implications
End with what the viewer should now believe, do, watch for, or question. Keep it grounded. Avoid fake certainty where the evidence is still moving.

## Research standards

- Cite sources in `storyboard.md` as source notes, but keep `narration.txt` clean for TTS.
- Distinguish facts from interpretation.
- Do not overclaim from one study, anecdote, or benchmark.
- For current topics, gather fresh source material before writing.
- If evidence is weak or mixed, make that uncertainty part of the narrative.
